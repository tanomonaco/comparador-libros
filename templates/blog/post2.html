How to Save Money When Buying Books Online

Posted on July 15, 2025

A passion for reading is one of life’s greatest joys, but it can quickly become an expensive hobby. Fortunately, being a book lover doesn't have to break the bank. With a few smart strategies, you can build your library without emptying your wallet. Here’s how.

Use a Price Comparison Website

The most important rule of online book shopping is that prices are not the same everywhere. The same exact book can have wildly different prices across various online stores. Instead of opening ten different tabs, use a price comparison tool that does the hard work for you. It instantly shows you the best available price, making it your first and most effective stop.

Embrace Used Books

Many readers want their books in pristine condition, but if you’re looking to save money, the used book market is your best friend. Stores like AbeBooks have millions of titles in "Very Good" or "Like New" condition for a fraction of the cost of a new copy. A gently used book tells the same story, after all!

Consider Different Formats

Are you set on a hardcover edition? While beautiful, hardcovers are always the most expensive option when a book is first released. Waiting for the paperback version can save you a significant amount. And if you have an e-reader, ebooks are almost always cheaper than their physical counterparts.

Hunt for Free Shipping

A low price can be deceiving if it comes with high shipping costs. Before you buy, always check the store's shipping policy. Many retailers offer free shipping once you reach a certain spending threshold (e.g., free shipping on orders over $35). Sometimes it’s worth adding another book to your cart to qualify for free shipping and get more value for your money.

By being a savvy shopper, you can make your reading budget stretch further than you ever thought possible. Happy reading!
